Introduction

Some terms:

  • MPCI: Multi-peril crop insurance. The largest in the US (444 million acres) that typically covers up to 75% of an APH with some areas allowed to cover up to 85%.
  • APH: Actual production history. Typically the average of the last ten years of crop yields.
  • Catastrophic loss: MPCI loss where the govenment steps in. Typically 25% loss.
  • Shallow loss: Other loss which may still (typically) incur a loss. Typically 15% loss.
  • 508h: Process by which new insurance products can be suggested like supplemental policies covering 85% to 75%.

Dictated by US Code 1508:

the level of coverage ... may be purchased at any level not to exceed 85 percent of the [expected] individual yield ... the yield for a crop shall be based on the actual production history for the crop

Study's alternative:

the level of coverage ... may be purchased at any level not to exceed 0.5 standard deviations below the [expected] individual yield ... the yield for a crop shall be based on the actual production history for the crop
