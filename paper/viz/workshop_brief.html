Practice-Agnostic Promotion of Resilience

System-wide Perspective on Multi-Peril Crop Insurance

‚ö†Ô∏è Crop Insurance is Under Stress

We saw:

  • The costs of crop insurance in the U.S. have already increased by 500% since the early 2000s with annual indemnities reaching $19B in 2022 (Schechinger 2023).
  • Retrospective analysis attributes 19% of "national-level crop insurance losses" between 1991 and 2017 to climate warming, an estimate rising to 47% during the drought-stricken 2012 growing season (Diffenbaugh et al. 2021)

We tried:

  • We built a simulation tool.
  • We looked at expected changes in growing conditions.
  • We ran models into the past as well as 1 decade vs 3 decades into the future.
  • We focused on corn in the US Corn Belt.
  • We tracked individual risk units and insurance policies.
Overview of Climate-driven Doubling of U.S. Maize Loss Probability: Interactive Simulation with Neural Network Monte Carlo. Published in JDSSV (Pottinger et al 2025). DOI: 10.52933/jdssv.v5i3.134.

üìà We See the Stress Getting Worse

  • Simulations see a riskier future: Yield losses which may trigger indemnity claims will not only be more severe around 2050 but also be twice as frequent.
  • We anticipate FCIP pressures: the traditional "75% of average APH yield" will likely become riskier to insure than in the past.
  • These results are seen even though we simulate changes in APH even without yield exclusions.
Summary of key findings showing the doubling of maize loss probability under climate change scenarios.

The core issue is that rate setting encourages aggregate yield but not additional yield stability.

üèõÔ∏è Policy Solutions

There are two ways to incorporate practice-agnostic incentives, rewarding outcome (stability) not method.

APH modification

  • Incorporate yield variability into APH calculations.
  • For example, move from 0.75 * APH to APH - std dev * 1.45 which maintains current claims rates but reduces future risk.
  • However, requires statutory change to APH structure

Stability modification:

  • Flat-rate premium discount for "high stability" growers
  • Similar to existing $5/acre cover crop payments
  • Amenable to 508h process by which RMA can pilot new approaches

‚è∞ Why This Matters Now

Policy Pressure Points

  • Budget constraints: Blumenauer bill caps subsidies at $125K
  • Cap proposals: $40K caps being discussed
  • Reinsurance pressure: Regional availability concerns growing for some specific policies.
  • Historical precedent: Trump previously proposed reducing subsidies from 62% to 48%

System Stability

  • Government-supported agricultural insurance crucial for food system stability
  • Preserve program viability through adaptation
  • Alternative to across-the-board cuts or caps

Climate change requires evolving our crop insurance approach from rewarding aggregate yield to rewarding both higher yields and higher stability.
